The complete eDiscovery certification overview!

Certificates are a popular means of improving your resume in the world of e-Discovery. When looking on LinkedIn, you will notice that many eDiscovery professionals certify in at least one specialty. Whether it's a vendor-agnostic course like ACED's eDiscovery specialist, or the challenging Relativity Certified Administrator, you will certainly enjoy the advantages of getting certified!

We've scoured the web for every single certificate which is currently available in eDiscovery and have created an overview with what we consider the most essential aspects of every certificate exam or course:

  • Pricing
  • Course/Exam Setup
  • Course/Exam Duration
  • Whether it's online or a test center location
  • Any mandatory training or courses
  • Any recommended prerequisites
  • If there are any CLE credits to be obtained
  • How to maintain the certificate,
  • The retake policy (if any)

You will notice that it's quite hard to compare the certificates with one another. For example, some certificates might require a 3-day course, while others will allow you to take the exam without any mandatory prerequisites. The pricing is also wildly different, ranging from tens to thousands of dollars.

The purpose of this page is, therefore, not to compare certificates one-on-one but for you to get a full overview of what's out there for the eDiscovery tools that you use on a daily basis, and your amount of experience in the industry.

Independent eDiscovery certification programs

We kick off the overview with two institutes that offer certification, which is not tied to a specific vendor or software, but seek to improve your general eDiscovery skill-set: ACEDS and Arkfeld's EDEC.

Association of certified E-Discovery Specialists (ACEDS) Certification

ACEDS (A BARBRI Professional Association) is a member organization for e-discovery professionals. The organization is vendor-neutral and has no commercial or financial ties. The membership for eDiscovery professionals includes, among other perks, a mentorship program and live webinars.

The ACEDS Certified E-Discovery Specialist exam is one of the most popular certificates in E-Discovery. For good reasons, as nearly 40 experts have collaborated to construct one of the most challenging exams in eDiscovery! In may 2021, a Canadian version has launched in Beta.

Since 2018, ACEDS offers a new certificate designed for e-Discovery professionals at all levels: The eDiscovery Executive Certificate.

In 2021, ACEDS together with the National Society of Legal Technology introduced a new training and certificate called the “eDiscovery Technology Certificate” (eDTech). This new training program provides law students and legal professionals with an interactive exploration of e-Discovery technology solutions

ACEDS Certified E-Discovery Specialist (CEDS)

The ACEDS 'CEDS' certificate is the first independent e-discovery certification program and covers a wide range of subjects, including: project planning, litigation hold, implementation, processing, and review.

Since 2010 the certificate has been one of the most popular exams to take and is definitely a worthwhile addition to your resume.

The exam is considered quite a challenge, with a passing grade of 66.4%, so make sure to prepare well by studying the materials and viewing the three 90-minute webinars planned every quarter.

CEDS Canada Certification (Beta)

Now in Beta with a 50% discount!

Beginning in May 2021, ACEDS is launching its flagship Certified E-Discovery Specialist (CEDS) exam and training program in Canada. Initially, ACEDS will offer a beta exam through August 1st, with substantially discounted rates for the training and certification exam.

The beta exam is the final critical step in the development of the CEDS Canada exam, which will launch later this year. Beta exam participants will take an extended-time exam with 160-170 questions. Once the beta exam closes in August, the results will undergo full statistical analysis. At the completion of the analysis, questions for the final exam are selected and a cut score will be established. Beta participants are graded using the same questions and passing score as the final version of the exam.

The beta exam is necessary to comply with industry standards for certification credentialing. This “testing of the test” will ensure that the CEDS Canada exam meets the same high standards ACEDS has had for the CEDS certification for over ten years.

Arkfeld's eDiscovery Training Center

Arkfeld is an organization that provides instructional content to the legal community in the areas of eDiscovery, digital evidence, and forensics, and data analytics.

Unlike most of the other companies on this page, Arkfeld offers the basic and advanced courses on a team subscription basis, where a full team can get certified for a fixed price per month. Arkfeld strongly believes that everyone who touches eDiscovery should have access to the same testing and training content.

Arkfeld offers a basic, advanced, and mastery class. Certification on every level!

Arkfeld EDEC EDiscovery Basic & Advanced Course (Group Subscription)

Arkfel'd subscription model gives you full unlimited access to 4 basic and advanced on-demand courses:

  • Basic - Information Technology (IT) for Legal Professionals
  • Basic - ESI Pretrial Discovery: Critical Issues, Strategy and Tactics
  • Advanced - Information Technology (IT) for Legal Professionals
  • Advanced - ESI Pretrial Discovery: Critical Issues, Strategy, and Tactics

The 'Information Technology for Legal Professionals Course' serves as an overview and essential primer of the field of computer and data forensics aiming to provide an understanding of how the law and the technological issues interconnect.

The 'ESI Pretrial Discovery: - Strategy and Tactics' course identifies and addresses the strategy and tactics for handling electronically stored information (ESI) issues throughout pretrial discovery. These include scope of discovery, preservation obligation and legal holds, controlling costs, search methodology, form of production, accessibility of ESI, limitations on discovery, clawback agreements, cost allocations and much more!

AccessData Certification

There's a big chance that you have run into AccessData's famous FTK images if you're an eDiscovery professional! AccessData is not only a leader in digital forensics but also offers the eDiscovery platform 'AD eDiscovery', an End-to-end e-discovery platform supporting preservation through production. AccessData has a wide range of certificates for all of it's products, one of which is aimed at the eDiscovery platform:

AccessData EDiscovery Certified Administrator (EDCA)

The AccessData eDiscovery Certified Administrator exam is specifically focused on the IT professional responsible for administering AD eDiscovery. The certification focuses on defensible data collection, practices in an e-discovery investigation, and how the use of AD eDiscovery can ensure electronic evidence can be self-authenticated to comply with Federal Rules of Evidence 902 and 902.

The EDCA exam is one of the most affordable exams in this overview, you can get certified for only $100.
